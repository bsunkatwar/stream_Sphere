The Basics

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help sites remember you, maintain sessions, and improve performance. They do not contain your name or contact details, but may include a unique identifier tied to your browser.

We also use similar technologies: web beacons (transparent pixel images), local storage objects (persistent browser data), and pixel tags (code snippets that report on actions). All are collectively referred to as "cookies" in this Policy.

Cookie Categories

Types of Cookies We Use

We use four categories of cookies, each serving a specific purpose.

Strictly Necessary
Always On

Essential for the Platform to function. Cannot be disabled. Required for login, forms, and security.

  • ss_session — Login session. Expires at browser close.
  • ss_csrf — CSRF protection. Session cookie.
  • ss_consent — Stores your cookie choices. 12 months.
Performance
Optional

Counts visits and measures traffic sources so we can improve performance. All data is aggregated and anonymous.

  • _ga / _ga_* — Google Analytics. 2 years.
  • ss_perf — Page load tracking. 30 days.
  • ss_abtest — A/B test assignment. 30 days.
Functional
Optional

Enable personalisation features like language, video quality, and dark mode preferences.

  • ss_lang — Display language. 12 months.
  • ss_theme — Dark/light mode. 12 months.
  • ss_history — Watch history. 30 days.
Advertising
Optional

Set by advertising partners to build interest profiles and measure ad effectiveness across sites.

  • _fbp — Meta Pixel. 90 days.
  • IDE — Google DoubleClick. 13 months.
  • ss_adid — Frequency capping. 30 days.

Additional Options

More Ways to Control Cookies

Browser Settings

Most browsers let you refuse or delete cookies via settings. Visit aboutcookies.org for browser-specific instructions. Note: disabling cookies may impair some Platform features.

Google Analytics Opt-Out

Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on to prevent data collection across all sites using Google Analytics.

Ad Interest Opt-Out

Opt out of interest-based advertising at youronlinechoices.com (EU/UK) or aboutads.info (US).

Mobile Device Settings

On iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. On Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads. Limit ad tracking directly from your operating system.

Do Not Track

Because there is no uniform standard for interpreting DNT browser signals, StreamSphere does not currently alter its behaviour in response to them. Use the controls above to manage your preferences.

Third-Party Content

Pages with embedded third-party content (maps, social buttons) may set cookies from those providers. StreamSphere has no control over third-party cookies; please review their own policies.

Policy Updates

We may update this Cookies Policy to reflect technology or legislation changes. Material changes will be announced via a Platform banner or email before the effective date.
